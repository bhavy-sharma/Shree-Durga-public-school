School Rules

Mandatory Guidelines for Students

General Rules:
    Attendance & Punctuality
  1. Students must attend school regularly and maintain at least 75% attendance. Late arrivals must report to the school office and provide a valid reason. Leave applications must be submitted in writing by parents/guardians.
  2. Every student must carry his/her Almanac to school each day.
  3. Students must come to school regularly and punctually.
  4. Students are not allowed to bring any type of objectionable literature/material/electronic gadgets to the school. The school reserves the right to inspect randomly and confiscate these items.
  5. Parents are requested to avail school bus facilities for the transportation of their wards. Non-bus commuters should arrive at school ten minutes before the start of the Assembly/School starting time.
  6. Latecomers will be sent back home and fined.
  7. The warning bell before a class/assembly is a signal for all to go to the assembly or to go to their respective classrooms. This should be done promptly and in silence.
  8. Changing classrooms between periods should be done in silence and in an orderly manner.
  9. Students must ensure that they carry the class pass whenever they are required to move out of the classrooms.
  10. No books (other than textbooks or library books), magazines, or newspapers should be brought to the school without the Principal's permission.
  11. Lending or borrowing money or other articles is not permitted.
  12. Students who come to school in the care of parents/guardians should never leave before their arrival. If delayed, they should report to the school office.
  13. Use of poly-bags is strictly prohibited in the school. Students should not use these for any purpose.
  14. Students are warned not to buy anything, especially eatables, from street vendors.
  15. Students should observe polite manners wherever they go. They should always remember that the school is judged by their conduct.
  16. Exploding crackers or splashing colors during Deepawali/Holi in the school premises is strictly forbidden.
  17. Monitors/Badge holders must wear their badges daily. In case of loss, a fine of Rs. 100 will be charged.
  18. Students who come late thrice in a month will be sent back home and fined.
  19. Students not in the school uniform will be fined. Students who are uniform defaulters three times a week will be sent back home and fined as well.
  20. A student returning to school after suffering from an infectious or contagious disease must produce a doctor's certificate permitting them to rejoin class.

The prescribed period of quarantine is:

Chickenpox: Six days after the disappearance of the first crop of vesicles.

Measles: Seven days from the appearance of the rash.

Mumps: Until the swelling of the salivary glands has subsided, which may be after nine days from the appearance of distinctive symptoms.

General Damages:

For all deliberate damages to furniture, library books, school property, and laboratory equipment, the defaulters will have to pay the actual cost of the damaged item and 10% of the cost as a penalty.

Writing or scribbling on the walls, furniture, or causing damage to school property in any form will be treated as a breach of discipline. Any such act shall entail punishment and recovery of the cost, even if it is done accidentally.

Code of Conduct:
  • Students must not ride in and around the school compound on bicycles.
  • Shouting, whistling, and use of abusive language is strictly prohibited.
  • Entry into the school premises with any weapon/ammunition is strictly prohibited.
  • Don’t litter the school premises and use the dustbins provided.
  • Possession of expensive or prohibited articles in the school will be treated as an offense.
  • Disrespect to any teacher or staff member will be treated as a breach of discipline.
  • Distribution of any sort of gift items or presenting gifts to teachers, staff, students, or any other member of the school is prohibited even on special occasions.
  • No student is allowed to attend any school function or event outside the school without the permission of the school.
